Slices of the Big Apple

New York City is one of the largest cities in the world, so naturally, the budget is just as enormous and complicated as the city itself.

This primer on the NYC budget aims to help you understand the very basics of the budget, then takes you through a 5-year analysis of just one of the many slices of our own Big Apple.

Who's involved in the process?

The NYC budget is required to be balanced, which means that we cannot spend more than we take in via taxes and other sources of revenue.

Let's review what we learned:

  • The budget is made of seven main components

Part Two: Chewing on the Slices

Now that you've gotten to know the budget a little better, let's dig in and figure out what it looks like close up.

Over the past five years, the budget has steadily increased—that is, until the COVID-19 pandemic.

Because the budget for 2020 was created in 2019, we hit peak allocation for this five-year period in 2020. This is why you see a reduction in total allocation for FY 2021.

Our first instinct might be to inspect these, but when we focus on the biggest department spenders, we tend to miss smaller stories that impact our communities and the way we live our daily lives. So let's try looking at something a little closer to the ground!

In New York City, we have several levels of government ranging from federal to state to city to community. At the closest level, our community government is comprised of our community boards.

Community boards are a group of elected members from your local community who decide what projects should be worked on to improve the community. They're responsible for some public works projects as well as initiatives and hearing concerns from people within their field of influence.

Why do community boards in Manhattan receive more funds than community boards in other boroughs? Is it systemic inequality or is it that the cost of living impacts even our own governments?

The answer is: both.
